## Canadian Registered Account Rules

### TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)
- Contributions: After-tax dollars
- Growth/income: Completely tax-free
- Withdrawals: Tax-free, room restored following calendar year
- Foreign dividends: Subject to withholding tax (e.g., 15% US withholding on US stocks) — TFSA does NOT exempt foreign withholding
- Best for: High-yield Canadian dividend stocks, REITs (no withholding)
- 2026 annual limit: $7,000 (indexed to inflation)

### RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)
- Contributions: Pre-tax (deductible), lowers taxable income
- Growth/income: Tax-deferred until withdrawal
- Withdrawals: Taxed as income (ideally in retirement at lower bracket)
- Foreign dividends: US-Canada tax treaty exempts US withholding tax on dividends in RRSP — NOT TFSA
- Best for: US dividend stocks (no 15% withholding in RRSP), higher-income years
- Annual limit: 18% of prior year income, max $32,490 (2025)

### FHSA (First Home Savings Account) — Since 2023
- Contributions: Tax-deductible (like RRSP)
- Growth: Tax-free if used for first home purchase
- Withdrawal for home: Tax-free
- Withdrawal for other: Taxed as income (can transfer to RRSP penalty-free)
- Annual limit: $8,000 | Lifetime limit: $40,000
- Carry-forward: Up to $8,000 unused room carries forward

## Withholding Tax Reference

| Source | Account | Withholding Rate | Recovery |
|--------|---------|-----------------|---------|
| US dividends | TFSA | 15% | Cannot recover |
| US dividends | RRSP | 0% | Treaty exemption |
| US dividends | Non-reg | 15% | Foreign tax credit |
| Canadian dividends | Any | 0% | N/A |

## Account Optimization Strategy

```
Priority for Canadian dividend investors:
1. FHSA first (if first-time buyer eligible) — double tax advantage
2. RRSP for US dividend stocks — avoid withholding tax drag
3. TFSA for Canadian high-yield stocks (REITs, banks)
4. Non-registered for remaining Canadian eligible dividends
   (dividend tax credit provides favorable treatment)
```

## Common Mistakes to Flag

- Holding US dividend stocks in TFSA (15% withholding, unrecoverable)
- Over-contributing to TFSA (CRA penalty: 1%/month on excess)
- Not tracking FHSA room carefully (2-year carry-forward limit)
- Withdrawing RRSP before retirement without considering income bracket impact
- Confusing TFSA room restoration timing (restored Jan 1 following year of withdrawal)
